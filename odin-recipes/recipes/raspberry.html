RASPBERRY CHEESECAKE

Raspberry cheesecake is a creamy dessert with a buttery crust, smooth cream cheese filling, and a vibrant, tangy raspberry topping.

  • 1 ½ cups crushed graham crackers (or digestive biscuits)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (optional)
  • 2 cups cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup sour cream (optional)
  • 1 ½ cups fresh or frozen raspberries
  • ¼ cup sugar (for the raspberry topping)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 1 tablespoon water

PROCEDURE.

  1. Prepare the crust: In a bowl, mix the crushed graham crackers and sugar (if using). Add the melted butter and stir until the mixture is well combined. Press the mixture into the bottom of a springform pan to form an even layer. Refrigerate while you prepare the filling.
  2. Make the cheesecake filling: In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and granulated sugar until smooth and creamy. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract and sour cream (if using), then mix until fully combined.
  3. Bake the cheesecake: Pour the cheesecake filling over the crust. Bake in a preheated oven at 325°F (163°C) for 50–60 minutes, or until the center is set and slightly jiggles when shaken. Allow the cheesecake to cool at room temperature before refrigerating for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  4. Prepare the raspberry topping: In a saucepan, combine the raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the berries release their juices and the mixture begins to simmer. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch and water to form a slurry, then stir it into the raspberry mixture. Cook for an additional 2–3 minutes, or until the topping thickens. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  5. Assemble the cheesecake: Once the cheesecake has chilled, pour the cooled raspberry topping over the cheesecake, spreading it evenly. Return the cheesecake to the refrigerator for another 30 minutes to set.
  6. Serve: Remove the cheesecake from the springform pan, slice, and serve chilled.
