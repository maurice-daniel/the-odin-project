Blueberry Cheesecake

Blueberries were once known as the "star of the dessert world." In fact, blueberry cheesecake is sometimes referred to as "the summer cheesecake" because blueberries are in season during the summer months and their sweet, tangy flavor is a perfect complement to the richness of cheesecake. Plus, they're packed with antioxidants, making this cheesecake not only delicious but also health-conscious!

Popular Cheesecake Varieties

Classic New York-Style Cheesecake

The original New York cheesecake is said to have been created by Arnold Reuben, a deli owner in the 1920s. He accidentally invented the recipe when he used cream cheese in a pie by mistake, and it became a hit. New York-style cheesecakes are distinct for their ultra-creamy texture, which is achieved by using a lot of cream cheese, giving them a rich, velvety consistency.

Combining the rich, creamy texture of cheesecake with the allure of chocolate made it a dessert fit for special occasions, and it's been a crowd-pleaser ever since.

Strawberry Cheesecake

Strawberries are one of the oldest cultivated fruits in the world, dating back to Roman times! Their sweet and tart flavor pairs perfectly with the creamy richness of cheesecake. Strawberry cheesecake has been especially popular in the U.S. since the 1970s when the "fresh fruit craze" hit desserts.

Lemon Cheesecake

Lemon cheesecakes have a long tradition in the Mediterranean, where citrus fruits were cultivated for centuries. The combination of the tart lemon and creamy cheesecake is thought to have originated as a way to balance the richness of dairy with the refreshing zest of citrus, making it a favorite spring and summer treat.

No-Bake Cheesecake

No-bake cheesecakes became popular in the mid-20th century, primarily because they were faster to make and didn't require an oven, making them a go-to dessert during the post-WWII era when people were looking for convenience in cooking.

Pumpkin Cheesecake

Pumpkin cheesecake has become so popular in the U.S. that pumpkin-flavored foods account for a $500 million market each year. It's most commonly associated with fall and Thanksgiving, but the combination of the spicy pumpkin filling and creamy cheesecake base makes it a year-round favorite for many.

Tiramisu Cheesecake

Tiramisu itself was created in Italy in the 1960s, and its name means "pick me up" due to the espresso's stimulating effects. Combining this iconic Italian dessert with cheesecake created a whole new experience, blending coffee, cream, and cocoa in a way that had never been done before.
